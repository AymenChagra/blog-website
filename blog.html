Subject: Computer Hardware

This artical offers a basic understanding of the term "hardware" and defines the different components of computer hardware and their role.

Computer hardware refers to the tangible, physical parts of a computer system. It encompasses a wide range of physical components that work together to enable the functioning of computers from processing, storing, and interacting with digital information.

A simple way to categorize the computer components is to divide them into internal parts which are essential for the computer to function and external parts which are primarily used for input, output, and user interaction.

The internal parts of the computer

  • The case: It's a plastic box that provides a secure and organized housing for the various hardware components and keeps them protected from external elements, such as dust, moisture, and physical damage.
  • The power supply: It's the part that plugs into the wall and provides all the other parts with the electricity needed
  • mother board: A wide flat board that all the components plug-in to, it's the part that let all the other components send electrical currents composing Data between each other. it's considered as the most important component.
  • CPU (Central Processing Unit or Processor): It's the component responsible for executing instructions and performing calculations necessary to run software applications. While it's not capable of storing too much Data, it's very good at doing things with Data: quickly reading it, arranging it, doing the massive calculations needed to run your programs this is where most programs essentially run from, you can think of it as the brain of the computer everything that happens in your computer goes through your CPU at some point.
  • Hard drive (Disk Drive or storage): it's where all of your data is stored, while hard drive can store lots of data it's relatively bad at accessing that Data quickly, because most of the hard drives are made of spinning disks it isn't going to be able to constantly give your CPU the information it needs to run certain programmes, but that where RAM comes in.
  • RAM (Random Access Memory): RAM is another form of storage, it stores the exact same type of data as the Hard Drive, but RAM sacrifices storage space for nearly instant accessibility, unlike the Hard Drive that sends the Data through a tiny thin wire, the RAM is arranged in long thin sticks that insert into your mother board. When you ran a program or a project file your CPU identifies which part of Data are needed for that program to run, it pulls them from your Hard drive and it stores them in your ram sticks for quick accessibility (this why when you start a game it has to load, anytime you see loading it's loading the Data that composes that level from your bulky Hard Drive into your RAM) From user perspective follow this rule of thumb: RAM allows you to run intensive programs while Hard Drive allows you to have more programs and storage.
  • Graphics Card (GPU or Graphic Processing Unit) It's possible for the mother board to display a three-dimensional world in to your monitor but it will not look good enough. That's where coms the Graphic Card, it's like a standalone computer itself dedicated for the sole task of figuring out what pixels need to light up on your screen, in what color and in what time.If you are playing a game that has a very busy and high texture world with lots of modules and different angles and colors, your CPU is the thing that creates that world, it knows where the stuff is and it does so with data that has been stored into your RAM which was loaded from the Hard Drive, but it's your Graphic Card that figures out what it's supposed to look like.

The external parts of the computer

The external devices can be divided into four types:

  • Input devices: Input devices allow users to input commands and data into the computer. Examples include keyboards, mouse, touchpads, microphones and scanners.
  • Output Devices: Output devices display or present information produced by the computer. Common examples include monitors, printers, speakers, and headphones.
  • Peripherals: This category includes various devices that connect to the computer to expand its functionality, such as external hard drives, USB flash drives, webcams, and external sound cards.
  • Networking Devices: Devices such as routers, modems, and network adapters enable network connectivity and communication with other computers or the internet.

About the future of computer hardware

Computer hardware is expected to become more sophisticated and advanced in the next coming years. Technological advancements and innovations continuously push the boundaries of what is possible in terms of computer hardware capabilities. In the next few years, we can expect advancements in a lot of areas such as Processing Power, Memory and Storage, Graphics, Connectivity, energy-efficient designs and power-saving technologies.
